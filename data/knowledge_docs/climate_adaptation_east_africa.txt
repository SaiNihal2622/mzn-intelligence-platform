PROJECT REPORT: Community-Based Climate Adaptation in East Africa
=================================================================

Project ID: MZN-2024-EA-001
Client: East African Development Authority (EADA)
Region: East Africa (Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda)
Sector: Climate Adaptation & Resilience
Duration: 18 months (January 2023 – June 2024)
Budget: USD 2.4 million
Funded by: Green Climate Fund (GCF), SIDA

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------
This project supported 14 rural communities across Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda in
building resilience to climate variability. The intervention focused on drought-resistant
agriculture, rainwater harvesting infrastructure, and climate-smart livelihood
diversification. Over 12,000 smallholder farmers were trained in conservation agriculture
techniques, and 47 community water harvesting systems were installed.

KEY INTERVENTIONS
------------------
1. Climate-Smart Agriculture Training
   - Introduced drought-tolerant crop varieties (sorghum, millet, cowpea)
   - Established 28 farmer field schools across three countries
   - Trained 340 lead farmers as community climate champions
   - Distributed climate information via mobile-based advisory services

2. Water Harvesting Infrastructure
   - Constructed 47 rainwater harvesting systems (rooftop and surface runoff)
   - Rehabilitated 12 community boreholes with solar-powered pumps
   - Trained 90 community water management committees
   - Developed water resource management plans for each target community

3. Livelihood Diversification
   - Established 22 village savings and loan associations (VSLAs)
   - Supported 180 women-led enterprises in climate-resilient value chains
   - Connected producer groups to regional markets for drought-tolerant crops

RESULTS & IMPACT
------------------
- 78% of target households reported improved food security
- 45% reduction in water collection time for women and girls
- Average crop yield increase of 32% through conservation agriculture
- 94% of water committees remained operational at endline
- USD 120,000 in cumulative savings mobilized through VSLAs

LESSONS LEARNED
------------------
- Community ownership is critical: projects with local governance structures showed
  3x higher sustainability rates
- Gender-responsive design improved participation and outcomes
- Integration of traditional knowledge with scientific approaches enhanced adoption
- Mobile technology significantly reduced extension service costs
- Early engagement of local government ensures policy alignment

STAKEHOLDER LANDSCAPE
------------------
- National meteorological agencies (weather data partnerships)
- County/district agricultural offices (extension service collaboration)
- International NGOs: CARE, WFP, FAO (co-implementation)
- Local civil society organizations (community mobilization)
- Private sector: seed companies, mobile network operators

RECOMMENDATIONS
------------------
1. Scale conservation agriculture training to additional districts
2. Integrate climate risk insurance products for smallholder farmers
3. Develop regional knowledge-sharing platform for climate adaptation
4. Strengthen early warning systems with community-based monitoring
5. Explore carbon credit mechanisms for sustainable funding
