PROJECT REPORT: WASH Infrastructure & Capacity Building in West Africa
======================================================================

Project ID: MZN-2023-WA-009
Client: UNICEF West and Central Africa Regional Office
Region: West Africa (Ghana, Senegal, Burkina Faso)
Sector: Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)
Duration: 24 months (January 2022 – December 2023)
Budget: USD 4.2 million
Funded by: UNICEF, World Bank, AfDB

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------
This project strengthened WASH service delivery across 3 West African countries
through infrastructure development, institutional capacity building, and community-led
total sanitation (CLTS) approaches. The intervention reached 280,000 beneficiaries
across 120 communities, constructing or rehabilitating 156 water points, 3,200
household latrines, and 45 institutional WASH facilities in schools and health centers.

KEY INTERVENTIONS
------------------
1. Water Supply Infrastructure
   - Constructed 68 new boreholes with hand pumps
   - Rehabilitated 88 existing water points
   - Installed 12 solar-powered piped water systems
   - Established water quality monitoring protocols across all systems
   - Trained 156 community water management committees

2. Sanitation Infrastructure & Behavior Change
   - Implemented CLTS in 120 communities (92 achieved ODF status)
   - Constructed 3,200 household latrines with local artisan training
   - Built 45 WASH facilities in schools (gender-separated, disabilities-accessible)
   - Developed sanitation marketing strategies for sustained demand

3. Hygiene Promotion
   - Trained 600 community hygiene promoters
   - Distributed 15,000 hygiene kits during disease outbreak responses
   - Established handwashing stations in all target schools and health facilities
   - Created culturally adapted hygiene communication materials

4. Institutional Strengthening
   - Supported 3 national WASH sector coordination mechanisms
   - Developed asset management frameworks for WASH infrastructure
   - Created monitoring and evaluation systems using mobile data collection
   - Trained 90 government WASH officers in project management

RESULTS & IMPACT
------------------
- Access to improved water sources increased from 45% to 82% in target communities
- Open defecation reduced by 76% across project areas
- Diarrheal disease incidence decreased by 38% in children under five
- 92 out of 120 communities achieved Open Defecation Free (ODF) certification
- 94% of water systems functional at project end (vs. national average of 71%)

LESSONS LEARNED
------------------
- CLTS is most effective when combined with sanitation marketing and supply chains
- Gender-responsive WASH design increases utilization and sustainability
- Asset management frameworks prevent infrastructure deterioration
- Government co-investment signals ownership and improves sustainability
- Cross-border coordination is valuable in shared river basin contexts

STAKEHOLDER LANDSCAPE
------------------
- National WASH directorates (policy, regulation, coordination)
- UNICEF, World Bank, AfDB (funding, technical oversight)
- District/municipal governments (implementation, sustainability)
- International NGOs: WaterAid, IRC (co-implementation, learning)
- Local artisans and private sector (construction, supply chain)
- Community leaders and women's groups (mobilization, governance)

RECOMMENDATIONS
------------------
1. Scale solar-powered piped systems to replace hand pump infrastructure
2. Develop national WASH asset management information systems
3. Create sanitation enterprise models for private sector engagement
4. Integrate WASH and nutrition programming for compound impact
5. Establish climate-resilient water safety planning at community level
