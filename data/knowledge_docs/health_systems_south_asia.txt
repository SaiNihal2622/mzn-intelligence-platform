PROJECT REPORT: Health Systems Strengthening in South Asia
==========================================================

Project ID: MZN-2023-SA-007
Client: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Bangladesh
Region: South Asia (Bangladesh, Nepal)
Sector: Health Systems & Digital Health
Duration: 24 months (March 2022 – February 2024)
Budget: USD 3.8 million
Funded by: Global Fund, USAID, DFID

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------
MzN International provided technical assistance to strengthen primary healthcare
delivery systems across 6 divisions in Bangladesh and 3 provinces in Nepal. The
project focused on digital health information systems, community health worker
capacity building, supply chain optimization, and quality improvement frameworks.
The intervention reached approximately 4.2 million beneficiaries through 380
upgraded health facilities.

KEY INTERVENTIONS
------------------
1. Digital Health Information Systems
   - Deployed interoperable electronic health records across 380 facilities
   - Integrated DHIS2 dashboards for real-time disease surveillance
   - Trained 1,200 health workers on data entry and quality assurance
   - Established data governance framework aligned with WHO standards

2. Community Health Worker Program
   - Recruited and trained 850 community health workers (CHWs)
   - Developed mobile-based decision support tools for CHWs
   - Implemented performance-based incentive framework
   - Created referral pathways between community and facility levels

3. Supply Chain Optimization
   - Implemented last-mile distribution tracking system
   - Reduced essential medicine stockout rates from 34% to 8%
   - Established district-level supply chain coordination committees
   - Introduced demand forecasting models for seasonal disease patterns

4. Quality Improvement
   - Established quality improvement teams in 120 priority facilities
   - Implemented WHO Service Availability and Readiness Assessment (SARA)
   - Developed patient feedback mechanisms (SMS-based and community scorecards)
   - Created clinical mentorship program linking tertiary and primary facilities

RESULTS & IMPACT
------------------
- 62% improvement in data completeness across DHIS2 reporting
- 41% increase in antenatal care coverage in target areas
- 28% reduction in maternal mortality ratio (from 196 to 141 per 100,000)
- Essential medicine availability increased from 66% to 92%
- Patient satisfaction scores improved by 35 percentage points

LESSONS LEARNED
------------------
- Digital systems require sustained technical support beyond initial deployment
- Community health workers are most effective when integrated into formal health systems
- Supply chain improvements have immediate and measurable impact on service delivery
- Political economy analysis is essential for health system reform projects
- Interoperability standards must be established early to avoid data silos

STAKEHOLDER LANDSCAPE
------------------
- Ministries of Health (policy alignment, regulatory approval)
- WHO Country Offices (technical standards, coordination)
- UNICEF (immunization, nutrition integration)
- icddr,b (research partnership, evidence generation)
- Local health authorities (implementation, sustainability ownership)

RECOMMENDATIONS
------------------
1. Expand digital health systems to remaining districts with phased approach
2. Institutionalize CHW program within government health workforce
3. Develop national health data exchange standards
4. Create regional centers of excellence for quality improvement
5. Establish public-private partnerships for sustainable health financing
